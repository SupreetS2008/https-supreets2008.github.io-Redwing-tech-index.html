CAD Department

CAD includes: Inventor models, drawings, BOMs, constraints and material choices are all captured for reproducible builds.

Design Process

From concept to validated model

Using Autodesk Inventor, the CAD team developed structural and mechanical representations of the robot, defining constraints, manufacturing feasibility, and assembly relationships.

Early sketches (from the team notebook).
Mechanical Requirements

Design rules defined early

The notebook lists mechanical requirements including attachment points, weight limits, access for maintenance, and pathways for motion which guided component design and placement.

Specification pages and requirement checklists extracted from the notebook.
Mechanical Constraints

Ensuring real-world feasibility

Constraints were applied to define joint ranges, collision envelopes, and simulated motion paths to prevent unrealistic configurations and ensure manufacturability.

Constraint examples and collision checks included in CAD revision logs.
Subsystem Models

Subtractor, switch, and supporting assemblies

Major subsystems such as the subtractor and switch were modeled and iterated on with dimensioned drawings; tolerances and fastener details were recorded for production handoff.

Assembly drawings and exploded-view renderings used by the build team.
Materials & Renderings

Bill of materials and final visualizations

The CAD deliverables include a BOM, material callouts, and renders to communicate finishes and assembly orientation to sponsors and fabricators.

BOM excerpts and final rendered views prepared for documentation and sponsor presentation.
